RoutineFlow ✧ Privacy with soul

🌱 effective from April 25, 2025 · last refreshed with care

🍃🧸 Hey there, habit shaper! We built RoutineFlow as a little peaceful corner — no data spies, no cloud lurking, no “your data is our product”. Everything you track stays right where it belongs: on your device, under your thumb. This policy is our honest promise, written like we’d tell a friend.

①

Zero-data harvest — seriously, zip.

We do not collect, store, or share any personal data. Not your name, email, IP address, or even a single habit snippet. All your streaks, checkmarks, journal notes, and goals live exclusively inside your device’s local storage. There’s no external database, no sneaky analytics server, no silent transmission. What you see in RoutineFlow is what stays with you — always.

◈ No sign-up walls. ◈ No “anonymous usage” shipped elsewhere. ◈ No tricks. Just you and your growth.

②

Simple permissions, full control

RoutineFlow only requests access when it truly serves you — and never beyond.

  • 🔔 Notifications — gentle reminders for your micro-habits. You’re the boss: enable or disable anytime in your phone’s settings. We never send silent notifications or track when you receive them.
  • 📁 Storage (optional) — used only when you decide to export a backup (to keep your habit history safe) or import a previously saved file. Your data never leaves the device unless you actively share a backup via email, AirDrop, etc. That’s your choice, 100%.

📌 No camera, no microphone, no location, no contacts — ever.

③

Your progress, seen only by you

Inside RoutineFlow, you’ll find beautiful charts & stats — “how many times did I meditate this month?” — all generated right on your device. We never receive that information. No third-party analytics (Firebase, Mixpanel, or any tracking SDK) is baked into the app. Zero crash-reporting that ties back to you. The only “analytics” that exist are the ones you look at for your own motivation.

💡 In simple words: your habit journey is a private diary, not a live broadcast.

④

No hidden guests, no trackers

We don’t use advertising SDKs, attribution tools, or external analytics. That means no Facebook pixel, no Google Analytics, no Crashlytics quietly logging your device info. The only moment another app gets involved is when you deliberately tap “Share RoutineFlow” to tell a friend — then your device’s native share sheet appears, but we never see who you shared it with or what you wrote. Purely mechanical.

✅ No trackers. ✅ No creepy cookies. ✅ No “legitimate interest” loopholes.

⑤

Keeping your little universe safe

Your habit records are stored using your device’s native file system with standard sandboxing — no internet transfer = no man-in-the-middle risk. For extra peace of mind, we encourage you to use your phone’s built-in security: passcode, Face ID, or fingerprint lock. If your device is encrypted and locked, your RoutineFlow data gets the same protection. And because we never store anything on our servers, there’s simply nothing for “us” to leak.

🔒 A quick tip: periodically export an encrypted backup (via manual option) and keep it somewhere safe — you own your history completely.

⑥

All ages welcome, no data mining

RoutineFlow is designed to be friendly for everyone — kids, teens, parents, grandparents. We don’t knowingly collect any personal information from children under 13 (or any age, actually). Since we don’t collect names, emails, or any identifiers, there’s no “children’s data” to manage or misuse. Families can feel safe without invasive advertising or profiling. If you’re a parent and have any questions, just reach out (contacts below).

🌈 We believe building good habits should feel light and safe, regardless of how old you are.

⑦

Living document, openly updated

From time to time, we might refine this Privacy Policy — to make it clearer, to align with new OS features, or to add more transparency. If we make a change, we’ll notify you via a friendly in-app notice (a small badge when you open RoutineFlow) and update the “Effective Date” above. We won’t ever reduce your privacy rights without your explicit consent. You can always find the latest version inside the app’s settings or on this screen.
